# The framing (read first ‚Äî the spine of the whole document)

Read this before the chapter list; every structural choice below follows from it. *(In the
thesis itself this is not a chapter; its content is distributed into Ch 1 (compressed) and
Ch 5 ¬ß5.1‚Äì5.4 (in full). It lives here, up front, because it is the planning keystone.)*

## F.1 What the thesis actually contributes ‚Äî the map, not the variants

The variants use **standard techniques** (Merkle commitments, grand products, blinded
commitments, in-circuit verification). "I built hierarchical TSP-ZK variants" is the weak,
exposed claim. The durable contribution is the **framework**, and the variants are the
**evidence that instantiates it**:

1. **The dualism** ‚Äî decomposing a non-local problem gives *no* ZK speedup; the only payoff
   is parallelism / per-prover memory. (A negative result with a structural cause: the
   partition check the recombiner must restore exactly cancels the per-segment saving ‚Äî the
   NP asymmetry between *finding* and *checking*; ZK does no searching.)
2. **The stitching tax** ‚Äî recombining K independent segment-proofs into one sound, private
   statement is a single artifact with three coupled symptoms: **partition leakage**, **O(K)
   verifier cost**, **verifier-side bookkeeping**. Generated by two decisions: *where* the
   stitch is enforced (external / in-circuit / deferred) √ó *what* is stitched (plaintext /
   hiding commitment / witness).
3. **The frontier** ‚Äî the **pick-two triangle** at fixed privacy: **P** (parallel + low
   per-prover memory) / **V** (O(1) verifier) / **C** (low prover overhead). Each built
   architecture gives exactly two; folding (future) is the predicted empty corner.
4. **The methodology** ‚Äî controlled, equal-privacy comparison; one variable per step; the
   2√ó2 factorial that de-confounds mechanism from aggregation.

> **Terminology (thesis-facing).** Thesis prose uses **stitching tax** for the recombination
> cost and reserves *binding / hiding* for commitment-scheme properties (`STYLE_GUIDE ¬ß14.3`).
> Some internal planning docs still say "binding tax" ‚Äî same concept.

## F.2 The variant set ‚Äî the naming scheme and the grid

The in-scope set is a small grid with two axes. **The vertical axis is the contribution; the
horizontal axis is a lever the vertical axis forces you to pull.** Every variant is named
`{regime}-{mechanism}` so the name pins its cell (full convention in ¬ßN below).

| Stitching regime (‚Üì = the stitching tax) | **sort** mechanism | **product** mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| flat, monolithic (K=1) | `flat-sort` | `flat-product` |
| hierarchical, **plain** stitch | `plain-sort` | `plain-product` |
| hierarchical, **committed** stitch | `committed-sort` | `committed-product` |
| recursive, **in-circuit** stitch | `recursive-sort` *(control)* | `recursive-product` *(shipped)* |

- **Vertical = the stitching tax** (*where/what is stitched*: flat ‚Üí plain ‚Üí committed ‚Üí
  in-circuit). This is the focus, the privacy axis, the frontier.
- **Horizontal = the fingerprint lever** (*how the partition is checked*: deterministic sort
  vs probabilistic grand-product + Fiat‚ÄìShamir). **Not** a co-equal axis ‚Äî it is a consequence
  of the vertical axis (see ¬ßF.3).

Out of scope for the narrative (kept in the repo, mentioned only as alternatives tried):
`flat_full_presence`, `flat_full_invperm`, `flat_merkle_presence`, the disclosing-sub-matrix
"Variant B". The flat permutation ancestors `pairwise` / `sort` survive as the ¬ß4.2 mechanism
study. `flat-product` and `recursive-sort` are **instrumental controls** (Ch 6 factorial),
off the frontier figure.

## F.3 The fingerprint lever ‚Äî the second structural finding, subordinate to the first

Nobody set out to compare two permutation mechanisms. The grand product *appears* as a
**forced response to the stitching tax**, and its different soundness *falls out* as the
price ‚Äî it was never an independent design goal:

1. Decompose ‚Üí each segment must **publish and stitch a partition fingerprint**.
2. The naive fingerprint is the **sorted node set**, inherited from flat's sort check. But
   under decomposition it costs **O(M) public surface per segment** and a **serial** O(N)
   merge in the recombiner ‚Äî it *worsens* the O(K)-verifier symptom.
3. The **grand product is the lever on that symptom**: it collapses each segment's shared
   surface from **O(M) ‚Üí O(1)** (one field) and is **distributable** (each segment multiplies
   locally; the recombiner combines K fields).
4. **The price is not sought, it falls out:** the exact, structural multiset check becomes a
   probabilistic one (Schwartz‚ÄìZippel over a Fiat‚ÄìShamir challenge, sound only in the ROM).
   **Structural soundness ‚Üí computational soundness is the cost of the surface win.**

> **One-sentence statement (the named subsection ¬ß5.3):** the sort‚Üíproduct move is the prover's
> lever on the surface / O(K) symptom of the stitching tax ‚Äî it buys an O(1), distributable
> fingerprint and pays in soundness flavor; the same lever, the same trade, at every level.

**Why `flat` is the perfect control, not a retroactive add-on.** At K=1 the surface is already
O(1) = `{root, T}`, so the product's surface benefit is **zero** ‚Äî pulling the lever there is a
*strictly worse* trade (you pay the soundness cost for no surface gain). So the product is never
*chosen* at flat; it is introduced there only as **the next permutation mechanism** in the ¬ß4.2
study (pairwise ‚Üí sort ‚Üí ‚Ä¶ ‚Üí grand-product), where it isolates the mechanism's *bare cost* (the
witness-time inversion, ¬ßF.5) with its benefit switched off. Its later relevance to decomposition
is a **callback, not a forward-reference debt**: the product is independently motivated as a
permutation mechanism, and the factorial simply *benefits* from a circuit we would have built
anyway.

## F.4 The lever's meaning changes down the stitching axis (this is what makes it not repetition)

The lever is read four times under four different conditions; each reading is a distinct result,
which is why it earns a named subsection rather than a footnote:

| Stitch regime | What the sort‚Üîproduct lever *means here* |
|---|---|
| **flat** (`flat-sort`‚Üî`flat-product`) | Pure mechanism cost (witness inversion). Surface benefit = 0 ‚üπ product is a *strictly worse* trade ‚üπ the clean control. |
| **plain** (`plain-sort`‚Üî`plain-product`) | Lever goes *live*: O(M)‚ÜíO(1) surface + distributable, paid in soundness. **F7: neither dominates** (`plain-sort` cheaper total gates + deterministic; `plain-product` compact + recurses flat). |
| **committed** (`committed-sort`‚Üî`committed-product`) | **Equal-privacy finding:** both reach the *same* privacy rung ‚üπ the lever is now a **privacy-neutral pure cost/soundness choice**. |
| **recursive** (`recursive-sort`‚Üî`recursive-product`) | Hiding is *off the table* (both partitions are witness) ‚üπ the lever is **soundness-only + outer-surface**: O(M) inner ‚Üí outer grows with N; O(1) inner ‚Üí outer stays flat at ~704k. **This is why `recursive-product` is shipped.** |

## F.5 The two comparison readings + the forbidden move (the methodology)

Every comparison changes exactly one variable. The grid (¬ßF.2) makes the discipline literal:

- **Down a column = the stitching tax** (mechanism held fixed ‚üπ a cost delta is pure
  aggregation/stitch, never secretly a mechanism delta).
- **Across a row = the fingerprint lever** (stitch held fixed ‚üπ pure sort-vs-product: the
  witness inversion + the F7 non-domination result).
- **The diagonal is forbidden** ‚Äî e.g. `flat-sort ‚Üî recursive-product` confounds *both* changes.
  Naming this as off-limits is half the methodology contribution.

The **2√ó2 factorial** {flat, recursive} √ó {sort, product} is the device that proves the two axes
are **separable** (‚âàadditive, no interaction) ‚Äî the dualism, quantified. The clean headline
comparison is `flat-product ‚Üî recursive-product` (differ in *exactly* structure, no soundness
caveat to carry).

**The witness-time inversion (the row finding at flat).** `flat-sort` and `flat-product` share
GROUP 3 (Merkle) and GROUP 4 (threshold) byte-for-byte, take the identical Prover.toml, expose
the identical `{root, T}`. Measured (`results/flat.csv`): the product compiles to **more**
constraints (+3.5‚Äì7.9%, shrinking with N) yet solves its witness **faster** (up to **‚àí44%** at
N=1000, widening with N). Cause: the sort path's `check_shuffle` does ~2N **dynamic-ROM** array
reads (data-dependent indices ‚Üí memory-consistency machinery the witness solver must resolve);
the grand product is pure straight-line, statically-indexed arithmetic. Both land in the same
UltraHonk dyadic bucket, so proof size (14 656 B), prove time, and memory are unchanged. *Gate
count and witness-solving cost are not the same currency.*

## F.6 Refrains to repeat throughout the decomposition chapters (honesty caveats)

- **Total work is conserved** ‚Äî hierarchical never beats flat on *total* gates; the win is
  *parallelizability* of the same work (the dualism).
- **`plain-product` is never motivated on hiding** ‚Äî it still leaks (the `P_i` oracle); it earns
  its place on surface + soundness + the recursion bridge.
- **"Perfect hiding" of flat/recursion is *structural*, not information-theoretic** ‚Äî the public
  surface carries no partition info. UltraHonk-ZK is computational/statistical and *identical
  across all variants*; it is not a discriminator.
- **The K√ó parallel speedup is PROJECTED, not yet measured** ‚Äî the isolation benchmark
  (`ISOLATION_BENCHMARK.md`, `--isolated` harness flag) closes this; the one gap before submission.

# ¬ßN ‚Äî The naming convention (load-bearing handles, reused verbatim)

Three levels. Defined once here; reused without drift (`STYLE_GUIDE ¬ß14.3`).

**Level 1 ‚Äî the variant: `{regime}-{mechanism}`.**
- regime ‚àà `flat` ¬∑ `plain` ¬∑ `committed` ¬∑ `recursive` (the stitching tax, top to bottom)
- mechanism ‚àà `sort` ¬∑ `product` (the fingerprint lever)

**Level 2 ‚Äî the component (only decomposed variants have more than one circuit):**
- **segment** ‚Äî proves one M-node leg of the cycle; the unit of parallelism.
- **glue** ‚Äî a *separate* proof the verifier checks alongside the K segments (external stitch;
  used by `plain-*`, `committed-*`).
- **outer** ‚Äî a *recursive* proof that verifies the K segments **in-circuit** (used by
  `recursive-*`); the segments it consumes are its **inner** proofs.

`flat-*` is monolithic (no component prefix). `recursive-*` reuses the `plain` segments as
inners ‚Äî there is no distinct recursive segment, and its recombiner is an `outer-`, not a glue:
`recursive-product` = `segment-plain-product` inners + an `outer-product`.

**Level 3 ‚Äî what each segment publishes (its share of the public surface):**
- **node-set fingerprint** (`sorted_nodes[M]`) ‚Äî `*-sort` segments.
- **product fingerprint** (`P_i`) ‚Äî `*-product` segments (+ **chain anchors** `h_in/h_out`).
- **segment commitment** (`C_i`) ‚Äî `committed-*` segments: a blinded wrapper around the fingerprint.
- standing objects: **endpoints** (`start/end`), **partial cost**.

**Old ‚Üî new ‚Üî code mapping** *(code identifiers are unchanged):*

| `plain-sort` | **A** | `segment-plain-sort` | `glue-plain-sort` | `hierarchical_segment` / `_glue` |

**The framework (the conceptual contribution):**
- ¬ß5.1 **The dualism** ‚Äî recall ¬ß2.4.3 (decomposition is an optimizer's win); the **gate-count
  cancellation** (hierarchical Merkle saves no total gates; the O(N) partition check + K boundary
  Merkle proofs absorb the per-segment saving); the NP asymmetry and why it does *not* transfer
  (ZK has no search to reduce); embarrassingly-parallel vs algorithmic speedup. *The film to ¬ß1.3's
  trailer.* **Chorus: total work is conserved.**
- ¬ß5.2 **The stitching tax** ‚Äî one artifact, three symptoms (partition leak / O(K) verifier /
  bookkeeping); the two generating decisions (*where* √ó *what*). Seed the reveal: the O(K)-verifier
  symptom will turn out to be the *cheap* way to pay.
- ¬ß5.3 **The fingerprint lever** *(named subsection ‚Äî the second structural finding)* ‚Äî ¬ßF.3 in
  full: decomposition forces a partition fingerprint; sort = structural + O(M)/segment + serial;
  product = O(1) + distributable + computational; the price falls out of the surface win. State the
  lever once; the walk below collects its four readings (¬ßF.4).
- ¬ß5.4 **The pick-two triangle** ‚Äî P / V / C; each architecture gives two; folding the empty corner.
  The frontier *is* this triangle at fixed privacy. (Folding's full treatment ‚Üí ¬ß7.6.)

**The controlled walk (the evidence):**
- ¬ß5.5 Common architecture ‚Äî K segments + recombiner; independent composition; N-divisibility; K
  parameterisation; the external cross-check as a minimal sound stitch layer (and why it expands the
  *trusted-code* surface, not the *cryptographic* trust base). Pseudocode inline; **toolchain
  extensions** noted here (the `--hierarchical*` builder modes, the parallel harnesses) rather than
  re-describing the ¬ß4.4 infrastructure.
- ¬ß5.6 **`plain-sort`** *(the diagnosis)* ‚Äî decompose, stitch externally on plaintext; each segment
  publishes a node-set fingerprint + endpoints + partial cost; the glue sorts the concatenated N
  nodes, verifies K boundary Merkle proofs, sums ‚â§ T. Deterministic (the sort *is* the multiset
  check). Cheapest total gates of the family; **the partition is disclosed** (the tax, raw).
- ¬ß5.7 **`plain-product`** *(the lever, live; the Chekhov's gun)* ‚Äî sort/O(M) ‚Üí grand-product/O(1):
  each segment derives an FS challenge via a Poseidon2 hash-chain and publishes one product
  fingerprint `P_i` (+ chain anchors); the glue combines K fingerprints (O(K), not O(N)). **Not
  cheaper** ‚Äî it relocates the O(N) work into the segments (F7: neither dominates). Motivated on
  surface + soundness + the recursion bridge, **never on hiding** (still leaks via the unblinded
  `P_i` oracle). *The gun: its O(1), M-independent surface pays off only when something verifies the
  segment in-circuit ‚Äî hold that thought.*
- ¬ß5.8 **`committed-sort` / `committed-product`** *(the twins ‚Äî present together)* ‚Äî plaintext ‚Üí
  **blinded commitment**: each segment publishes one `C_i = fold(r_i, [values])`; the glue takes
  values+openings as witness and re-checks in-circuit; the verifier cross-check collapses to opaque-
  blob equality (ZK for free). The leak closes (reveals only K commitments); only the O(K)-verifier
  symptom remains. **The equal-privacy finding:** both reach the *same* rung (multiset
  computational, interior order info-theoretic, reveal K) ‚Äî they differ only in glue cost/mechanism.
- ¬ß5.9 **Recursion** *(the final form ‚Äî the climax)* ‚Äî external stitch ‚Üí **in-circuit (witness)**:
  the `outer-product` takes the K `segment-plain-product` inners and their public inputs as private
  witness, verifies them in-circuit, and re-runs the recombiner logic on those now-trusted-but-
  private values. Public surface collapses to `{root, T}` ‚Äî the perfect-hiding endpoint, equal to
  flat. **The gun fires:** `plain-product`'s O(1), M-independent surface keeps the outer
  segment-size-independent (~704k gates whether N=8 or N=480); an O(M) inner would grow with N ‚Äî
  which is **why `recursive-product` is shipped**. The **Fiat‚ÄìShamir floor** (the in-circuit
  recomputation of the inner transcript is the bulk of the ~704k gates; present for *any* inner; *is*
  recursion's stitching tax). `recursive-sort` is the off-frontier **control** (soundness/surface
  trade, not hiding ‚Äî both partitions are witness either way).
- ¬ß5.10 **Privacy analysis** *(the privacy ladder, as the local axis)* ‚Äî per-variant quantitative
  bound (10.3 bits at N=8 for `plain-sort`; the general formula); the assumption-decreasing ladder
  (disclosing ‚Üí `plain-product` oracle ‚Üí committed (Poseidon) ‚Üí committed (Pedersen) ‚Üí
  recursion/folding/flat); the commitment taxonomy box (Poseidon computational-cheap vs Pedersen
  unconditional-costly).
- ¬ß5.11 **Soundness of the constructions** *(TODO ‚Äî proof plan)* ‚Äî knowledge soundness as a
  *reduction* (assume UltraHonk KS + Poseidon2 CR/ROM + commitment binding + recursion KS; compose,
  do not reprove UltraHonk). One Œµ-bound theorem per variant: Œµ ‚â§ Œµ_SNARK + Œµ_FS + Œµ_SZ (+ Œµ_bind).
  Honest scope: flat / `plain-sort` / `committed-sort` fully provable; the product + recursive
  variants modulo cited FS-in-ROM + recursion KS; ROM-free FS, mechanized proofs, and Noir-circuit
  formal verification named as limitations.
